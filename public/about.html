Our Mission

We created this AQA GCSE History revision platform to provide students with high-quality, accessible study materials that align directly with the AQA examination board requirements. Our mission is to help students develop the knowledge, skills, and confidence needed to excel in their GCSE History examinations.

We believe that understanding history is not just about memorizing dates and events, but about developing analytical thinking, evaluating different perspectives, and making well-supported judgments—all skills that will serve students well beyond their exams.

AQA GCSE History: America 1920s-1950s

This site focuses on the AQA GCSE History period study of "America, 1920-1973: Opportunity and Inequality." This fascinating period covers:

  • The economic boom of the 1920s
  • The Great Depression and the New Deal
  • Post-war America, including the economic recovery
  • American society and culture in the 1950s and 1960s
  • Civil rights movements

Our current content focuses primarily on the first part of this period study, covering America from the 1920s through the 1950s, with particular emphasis on economic developments and their social impacts.

Examination Information

The AQA GCSE History assessment consists of two papers:

Paper 1: Understanding the Modern World

  • Section A: Period study (America, 1920–1973: Opportunity and inequality)
  • Section B: Wider world depth study
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Marks: 84 marks (including 4 for spelling, punctuation and grammar)
  • Weighting: 50% of GCSE

Paper 2: Shaping the Nation

  • Section A: Thematic study
  • Section B: British depth study with the historic environment
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Marks: 84 marks (including 4 for spelling, punctuation and grammar)
  • Weighting: 50% of GCSE

Our platform focuses on the America, 1920-1973 period study which appears in Paper 1, Section A.

Question Types

In the AQA period study, you will encounter these question types:

Mark Value Question Type Requirements
4 marks Describe two features Identify two features and support with specific factual detail
8 marks Explain the importance/significance Explain the importance of an event/development using specific knowledge
12 marks "How far do you agree" Present a balanced argument considering multiple perspectives
16 marks Interpretations question Evaluate a historical interpretation using detailed contextual knowledge

Our practice questions give you experience with all these question types and our AI feedback is tailored to the specific requirements of each.
