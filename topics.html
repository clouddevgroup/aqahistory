The Boom of the 1920s

The 1920s was a decade of economic prosperity in America, often referred to as the "Roaring Twenties." This period saw significant industrial growth, technological advancements, and cultural changes.

  • Mass production and consumer society
  • The automobile industry and Henry Ford
  • Advertising and hire purchase
  • Entertainment and popular culture
  • Social changes and tensions

The Great Depression

The Wall Street Crash of 1929 led to the Great Depression, a severe worldwide economic downturn that lasted until the late 1930s. It was the longest and most severe depression experienced by the industrialized Western world.

  • Causes of the Wall Street Crash
  • Effects on industry, agriculture, and banking
  • Social impact and unemployment
  • Hoover's response and limitations
  • The human cost of the Depression

FDR and The New Deal

Franklin D. Roosevelt's New Deal was a series of programs, public work projects, financial reforms, and regulations implemented between 1933 and 1939 to respond to needs for relief, reform, and recovery from the Great Depression.

  • Roosevelt's election and the First Hundred Days
  • The First New Deal (1933-1934)
  • The Second New Deal (1935-1938)
  • Opposition to the New Deal
  • Impact and effectiveness of the New Deal

World War II

America's involvement in World War II transformed the nation's economy, society, and global position. The war ended the Great Depression and established the United States as a global superpower.

  • Pearl Harbor and U.S. entry into the war
  • Economic impact and industrial mobilization
  • Social changes during wartime
  • The Yalta Conference
  • The development and use of the atomic bomb

The Early Cold War

After World War II, tensions between the United States and Soviet Union led to the Cold War, a period of geopolitical tension that dominated international affairs for decades.

  • The Potsdam Conference
  • Truman Doctrine and Marshall Plan
  • The Arms Race and nuclear weapons
  • McCarthyism and the Red Scare
  • The Cuban Missile Crisis

The Space Race

The Space Race was a competition between the United States and the Soviet Union for supremacy in space exploration. It was a crucial aspect of the Cold War and led to significant technological advancements.

  • Sputnik and the beginning of the Space Race
  • NASA and American response
  • The race to the Moon
  • Apollo 11 and the Moon landing
  • Impact on technology and international relations
